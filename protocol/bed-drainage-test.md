# Protocol — Bed drainage (perc test)

Does the bed drain below the root zone, or does a hardpan / high water table perch water. All beds, all crops. Gates any leach pass (salt relocates instead of leaving) and any waterlogged-bed call (sulphur → H₂S root burn).


## Kit

- Rod / rebar / screwdriver shaft (probe)

- Spade

- Ruler

- Water + timer

- Log sheet


## Quick tells (do first, ~10 min)

1. Probe straight down → smooth = fine; sudden hard stop at a consistent depth = compacted pan.

2. Open a 30–40 cm pit a few hours after irrigation → look for:

   - sharp wet-above / dry-below line → water not passing.

   - grey / blue-grey colour or rusty mottles → chronic waterlogging.

   - sour / rotten-egg smell → anaerobic.


## Perc test (the number, ~half a day)

1. Dig a hole **~30 cm deep**, ~15 cm wide, representative spot.

2. Pre-soak → fill, let drain fully once (measures steady-state, not first-fill suction).

3. Refill to a marked level.

4. Measure drop over **30 min** → ×2 = mm/hr.


## Overnight backstop (simplest pass/fail)

- Fill the 30 cm hole at end of day → empty by morning = drains; still standing = perched.


## Read

| Drop rate | Verdict | Action |
|---|---|---|
| > ~25 mm/hr | drains freely | proceed |
| ~10–25 mm/hr | adequate | proceed; space passes |
| ~2–10 mm/hr | slow | smaller passes, longer dry-back |
| < ~2 mm/hr, or stands overnight | perched / hardpan | **fix first** — broadfork / subsoil the pan, or address the water table |


## Record

- Date, bed ID, drop rate (mm/hr) or overnight pass/fail, pit observations.


## Notes

- Pans are patchy → test more than one spot per bed.

- Greenhouse beds get no rain flush → a marginal pan a field would mask shows up here; test before relying on leaching.

- cert 1 — rate thresholds are FAO/septic-perc rules of thumb, not Décembre-calibrated.
