# Domain — spinach (irrigation)


## Model


### Soil-moisture tension

- soil tension < ~10 kPa → pore space water-filled → root-zone anoxia → uptake stops   (soil physics; crop-independent saturation floor)

- spinach rooting depth ~10–15 cm → small water buffer → tension rises fast under transpirational load

- peak sun → peak transpiration → tension climbs toward the dry edge fastest

- at peak transpiration the limiter is root water-transport rate, NOT soil water content   (pushing below the saturation floor adds no delivery — it only displaces root-zone air)


### Leaf cooling

- overhead irrigation → free-water film on leaf → latent-heat evaporation → leaf cooling   (stomata-independent; cools at peak heat when stomata close / transpiration is supply-limited)

- film cools only while it holds → re-wet to sustain through peak sun


### Bolting

- bolting ← accumulated heat **+** long photoperiod   (Quebec summer ~15–16 h; can't be vented away)

- moisture stress → ↑ bolting   (contributor that shortens the clock, not the primary trigger)

- maximizing soil moisture does NOT prevent bolting when heat / photoperiod drive it


### Disease

- free-water film + warmth + slow drying → downy mildew (*Peronospora*)   (leaf wetness is required for infection)


### Setpoints

| Factor | Target | Band | Edge |
|---|---|---|---|
| Soil water tension | ~10 kPa at peak sun, relax toward 15 by evening | 10–15 kPa | <10 → anoxia / waterlogged; >15 → drought stress + bolting risk |


## Boundaries

- Covers irrigation moisture + overhead-cooling only.

- Nutrient supply / demand, pH / EC bands, and a full bolting thermal-time model are not yet established for spinach.
