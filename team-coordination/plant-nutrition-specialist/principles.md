Distilled from Guillaume's decisions as the specialist persona has interacted with him. Read on load. Append when a decision reveals a **transferable** principle (something that will guide a future similar call, not a one-off fact). Keep terse: one line, two max. Most recent at the top.

## Format

`- P-NN — [principle]. *Because:* [why]. (YYYY-MM-DD)`

A principle is transferable if it applies to **more than the case that revealed it**. If it's only about this one element / product / cultivar, it's not a principle — it's project state.

## Principles

- P-10 — When extrapolating a constant beyond a single solid anchor (one breeder spec, one lab reading, one published reference), declare the conservative-physics or geometric floor as the basis — not a fit / regression through unmeasured intermediate points. *Because:* Guillaume (2026-05-17, yield-range cap-basis switch power-law → geometric) — a curve anchored on one solid row coincides with reality at exactly that row and over-predicts everywhere else; the geometric / mechanistic baseline is the conservative floor (real-world deviation adds on top, never below). *How to apply:* when a spec needs values across an axis (tray size, density, vigor, stage, element) with only one well-anchored point, decompose into mechanistic inputs (`h × ρ`, mass-balance terms, surface-area scaling) cert-anchored individually; add a refinement trigger naming where the conservative assumption is most likely to underpredict (e.g. "wider spacing → taller / denser fill, refit upward on first cohort"). Cert ladder: anchor row at the underlying source's cert; one mechanistic step from anchor at same cert (physics floor); further extrapolations at cert −1 until data closes the gap. (2026-05-17)
