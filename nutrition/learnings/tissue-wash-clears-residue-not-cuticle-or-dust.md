# 0001 — A pre-digestion leaf wash clears spray residue, not cuticle-bound or dust contamination

**2026-05-30.** Our tissue lab (Agro-Enviro-Lab) washes samples before
acid digestion. That decides how to read an *élevé* value on any panel:
a wash strips **loose surface salt**, so for a foliar-sprayed element a
still-élevé read means real tissue over-supply, not residue sitting on
the leaf. Confirmed on the tomato T5 panel — Mn 132 / Cu 12 ppm flipped
from "real or contamination?" to real (cert 3).

## What a wash does NOT clear

- **Cuticle-penetrated foliar** — sprayed Mn / Cu / B that crossed into
  the wax reads as tissue but isn't metabolic → magnitude slightly
  over-read for surface-applied elements. Keeps these reads at cert 3,
  not 4.
- **Dust / soil contamination on a plain water rinse** — the classic
  trap is **Fe** (and to a degree Mn / Al): adhered soil dust needs a
  dilute-acid + detergent wash, not a rinse. A high tissue Fe stays
  suspect even on a "washed" sample. (Moot on the T5 panel — Fe read
  *suff* there, so no dust signal.)

## How to apply

1. Élevé + we spray it + Fe-class dust ruled out → treat as real,
   review whether we're over-supplying (foliar + fertigation stack).
2. Élevé Fe → discount as likely dust unless the lab confirms an
   acid+detergent wash.
3. Recipe-built over-supply (e.g. sulfate-form S) is real regardless of
   wash — chemistry, not surface.

## Why this earns a learning

Naive reading is "washed → all surface gone → every read is real." That
over-trusts Fe and slightly over-trusts sprayed micros. Easy to
re-derive wrong each panel; cross-crop (tomato + lettuce), so it lives
here, not in one tomato doc.
