# 2026 tomato decline is one pH-lockout crisis, not three problems

2026-05-30. The reported triad — thin new growth, flower
abortion, botrytis in a dry house — all trace to the single
pH-7.4 lockout (P −90%, N −26%, Ca below resistance band; cert 4
tissue, see `nutrition/tomato/doc/tomato-t5-tissue-analysis-2026-05-23.md`).

## Decisions
- **Flower abortion is NOT boron** — tissue B 57 ppm is
  sufficient. Considered B because abortion is the textbook B
  symptom; rejected on tissue evidence. It's the P/energy
  starvation. Don't buy borax for it.
- **Priority frame = root-cause first, not speed first.** P
  starvation limits root mass, throttling N + Ca uptake, so P is
  upstream. Subgoals ordered P → N → Ca even though P is slowest
  to move (multi-cycle pH drawdown). Considered speed-first
  (N → Ca → P, fastest kg this season); rejected the *ranking*
  but kept the calendar insight: all three start now, P runs as
  a parallel slow clock. Full table:
  `nutrition/tomato/doc/tomato-yield-recovery-subgoals-2026-05-30.md`.
- **Cheap gauges replace monthly tissue for fast elements** —
  petiole sap NO₃-N (N, weekly) + pour-through pH (the lockout
  driver). Tissue ICP stays the quarterly anchor for P + Ca,
  which have no cheap proxy.

## Why it's worth keeping
Future readers will see N as the obvious "vigour" lever and reach
for it (or for boron on the abortion) — this records why P/pH is
the real ceiling and why the ordering is causal, not fast-first.
