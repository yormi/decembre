# Protocol — AMF inoculation (SG1 interim P-uptake bridge)

Deliver P by direct mycorrhizal uptake while the sulphur drawdown
slowly frees the soil P vault. Mechanism + 4/5 rating:
`../../tomato/learnings/0002-interim-root-uptake-levers-during-ph-wait.md`.
Candidates + cert: `../sg1-free-p.md § Candidates`.

**On-hand:** none yet — this protocol compares both pre-purchase.

- Not mutually exclusive — the ideal play is DYNOMYCO drench now
  **and** AGTIV at the next transplant, but that's two purchases.
- If buying one: **DYNOMYCO** is the safer single buy (cert clear,
  works in both modes). AGTIV only beats it on local sourcing + spore
  count, and carries the open cert question.

**Don't stack both products** — not additive (cert 2). Both are built on
*R. irregularis* (AGTIV adds only a different strain of a fungus
DYNOMYCO already carries); colonisation of the root cortex **saturates**
(dose-response flattens, not linear) and the two compete for the same
infection sites. Real diversity would need a different *genus*, not a
second *R. irregularis* product — and DYNOMYCO already brings the
two-species mix (+ *G. mosseae*) in one cert-clear bag. Effectiveness is
raised more by root contact (transplant > drench), no competing high-P
fert/fungicide, warm soil — not by a second product.

## Two deployment modes

| Mode | When | Payoff odds | Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Transplant inoculation** (primary) | next tomato transplant | ~75% | granule in direct root/plug contact in the planting hole — the fungus needs to meet a new root |
| **Mid-crop root drench** (interim) | now, established crop | ~45% | suspend a wettable/soluble form in water, drench the root zone at the stem base |

Mid-crop colonisation of an established surface-mat root system is slow
— hence the lower odds. Worth it as a cheap bridge; the real win is at
transplant.

**One shot or repeat?** Depends on when:
- **Transplant:** single application. Once a root is colonised it's
  **permanent + self-spreading** — re-dosing a colonised plant is
  wasted (niche occupied, saturates).
- **Mid-crop bridge:** **2–3 passes, ~2–3 wk apart**, not one — and
  **cap at 3** on a T5 crop. Repeats don't stack on colonised roots;
  each catches the new root flush (contact is the bottleneck on an
  established mat). **Why 3 is the ceiling:** T5 is reproductive →
  new-root production is slowing (little fresh substrate for a 4th–5th
  pass), and colonisation self-spreads (if it takes, it takes in passes
  1–3). 4+ passes only pay on a young vegetative plant flushing roots.
  - **Interval ~2–3 wk** matches the tomato root-flush cycle. Tighter
    (weekly) = overlap on the same roots, wasteful. Wider (monthly) =
    misses flushes + stretches the bridge.
  - **Exit early if:** tissue P / vigour responds (colonised → stop),
    OR nothing moves ~4–6 wk after pass 1 (limiter is conditions, not
    count → redirect effort to the transplant dose).
  - Cumulative odds rise with **diminishing returns**; **never reaches
    the transplant ~75%**. Cost negligible at our scale (labour-limited).
  - **Cert 2–3:** the repeat-to-catch-flushes logic is mechanistically
    sound but thinly trialled (evidence base = single inoculation at
    planting). A cheap hedge, not a proven protocol — don't over-invest.

**Placement, not delivery, drives the odds.** Hand-drench vs drip
fertigation doesn't change the mode — the limiter mid-crop is
propagule-to-new-root-tip contact, not water distribution. Fertigation
is even + low-labour but stays ~45%, and only with a drip-compatible
form (SC P / REACH L), never the peat REACH P. The dollar that buys the
most confidence is the **transplant** dose, not a bigger/more-frequent
mid-crop one.

## Guardrails (both products)

- **No heavy soluble-P drench paired with AMF** — raising tissue P
  represses the mycorrhizal P pathway (the whole mechanism). Avoid
  high-P starter ferts in the same window.
- **Ocean fertigation (SG2) is compatible — do NOT stop it.** EZ-GRO
  Ocean 15-1-1 is only 1% P (not the high-P repressor) and its N is
  moderate organic amino-N. Fish hydrolysate is microbiome-friendly,
  not chlorinated, negligible CE. Complementary: Ocean grows root →
  more surface for AMF to colonise. Only avoid flushing the inoculum
  with a big irrigation pulse at the placement moment; resume daily
  fertigation right after.
- **No fungicide drench** near application — it kills the inoculant.
- **Water:** use the benign source water (CE 0.10); chlorinated water
  harms spores.
- **Soil temp >15 °C** for colonisation.
- **Cert before soil:** a brand OMRI/Ecocert claim is strong evidence,
  not the certifier's sign-off under CAN/CGSB-32.311 — clear it first.

## Monitoring

- Judge on **tissue P + new-growth vigour**, never a re-tested SME
  available-P number (AMF raises *tissue* P, not bulk soil P).
- AMF and the sulphur drawdown are complementary: AMF scavenges the
  available fraction harder now; sulphur enlarges that fraction later.
