## Why

Botrytis resistance (Ca-pectate cell-wall hardening) + stem
structure (Ca immobile → new growth + strength). Ca is
**uptake-limited**, not supply-short — solution Ca is abundant
(238 ppm, above optimal). So **feed-side Ca won't help**; the
fix rides on root recovery (SG1) plus root-bypass.

## Actions

### Root-bypass — foliar Ca (the only direct Ca lever now)
- Lever: CaCl₂·2H₂O foliar spray, 2×/wk, no surfactant
  (~17 mg Ca/m²/wk) — bypasses the impaired root path.
- Status: live in STORED foliaire (reintroduced 2026-05-28),
  operator timing via `/retire-recipe`.
- Cert: Ecocert-verified.

### Rides on SG1 — root recovery
- Ca uptake recovers as P/pH frees root growth (SG1). No
  independent soil-Ca action: adding Ca to a Ca-saturated
  calcareous soil does nothing.

### Indirect benefit — Ocean (SG2) + AMF (SG1)
- Both grow / extend the absorptive root system → more Ca
  uptake as a side effect. Not owned here; primary homes are
  SG2 (Ocean) and SG1 (AMF). No separate action — just expect
  some Ca lift as those land.

## Monitoring
- Tissue Ca (anchor); watch newest leaves/trusses (Ca immobile
  → old tissue won't recover).

## Open questions
- Whether foliar Ca alone holds the resistance band before SG1
  root recovery delivers the structural (stem) Ca that foliage
  sprays reach poorly.
