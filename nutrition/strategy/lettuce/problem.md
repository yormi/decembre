## The challenge

- Best ever **60 kg/bed** (harvest, **gross/untrimmed**) = ~60 g/head
  at 1000 heads. Target **200 kg** = ~200 g/head. Gap = **triple the
  head**, and it's real head mass — heads are physically small, not
  trimmed down. The plant simply isn't bulking.
- Bed: 30″ top + 12″ alley × 100 ft = 23.2 m², 1000 heads ≈ 43/m².
- **The paradox that frames it:** the 60 kg came from the **cold,
  no-input** bed. Heat + CO₂ + light did *worse*. The premium inputs
  are a net negative.

## The crux

**The bottleneck is the seedling — it's crap before it ever reaches
the field.** A plug that transplants weak, over-aged, and already
bolting cannot make a 200 g head; the field can only lose from there.
The cold no-input bed proves it: best field conditions + a crap plug
still top out at 60 g. **Fix the plug and you raise the ceiling;
nothing downstream matters until then.**

Why the plug is crap — three compounding faults in propagation:

- **Salted** — three marine feeds (Ocean + Acadie Poisson fish
  13 mL/L + kelp) concentrate in 1.25 L cells with no leaching →
  seedling Na 3166, tray EC 1.9–2.6 (lettuce wants ~1.0–1.5). The
  26 Apr fish bump (10→13 mL/L, "to push tray-finishing") made it
  worse.
- **Cooked** — propagation house too warm for a cool-season crop →
  bolting + mold in the trays.
- **Over-aged** — held to 5–8 wk though weight peaks at **4 wk**,
  then *declines* (16 g → 10 → 12). Transplanted on the way down,
  already flowering. Roots outgrow the 50-cell **because** of the
  hold, not because the cell is undersized — earlier "crowding"
  folds in here (`learnings/crowding-is-overaging-not-cell-size.md`).

Then the field **finishes** it: the weak plug goes into salty
calcareous soil (marine shrimp compost + CE creep → transplant
Na 4443, Ca/K/Mg blocked) and is run hot (heat lifts pH 6.9→7.3 →
lockout, drives more Na uptake). A crap plug in hostile ground can't
recover → 2-wk stall, no bulking.

**The unifying mechanism is sodium + heat.** Sodium is the
through-line (nursery feed *and* field soil — two ocean sources);
heat is the accelerant at both stages. Sodium hits three ways at
once: **osmotic** (can't drink in damp medium → growth stalls),
**ionic toxicity** (Na⁺ scorches leaf margins), **antagonism**
(Na⁺ blocks Ca/K/Mg → tipburn, soft, yellow). The dominant loss is
the osmotic stall + the short bulking window of a bolt-committed
plug; tipburn (low Ca) is a quality tax on top, not the main ceiling.

**So the diagnosis in one line:** the seedling is crap (salted,
cooked, over-aged), and a hot, salty field guarantees it
never recovers.

## What's critical vs noise

- **Critical:** sodium (osmotic stall + cation block), heat, plug
  quality. Fix these or nothing moves.
- **Noise / misreads to resist:**
  - "Feuilles jaunes" is **not** nitrogen — tissue N sufficient
    (3.87); yellowing is Mg blocked by Na.
  - **Not light-short** — adding light helped in deep winter only;
    in the warm season it just adds heat load.
  - **Not P** — lettuce P is fine/high (unlike tomato).
  - **Not pollination/variety** — it's environment + feed.

## Supply vs uptake

- **Ca / K / Mg — uptake-BLOCKED by sodium**, not supply-short.
  Feeding more won't help; the lever is **cut the Na + protect Ca**.
- **N — sufficient.** Adding fish-N is *negative* — the fish is the
  salt source.
- **P — adequate.** No action.
- **The growth stall itself** is osmotic (salt) + a short window
  (bad plug) — not a missing nutrient.

## Two clocks

- **Nursery de-salt + plug fix — FAST** (next sowing): cut fish,
  leach trays, bigger cell, transplant young + cool.
- **Field soil-salt drawdown — SLOW** (leaching over weeks–months;
  shares the tomato soil clock). Don't wait on it to fix the nursery.

## Bad strategy — the traps that feel like action

(Rumelt: bad strategy mistakes activity or goals for strategy and
fails to face the real constraint.)

- **"Add heat / CO₂ / light to push yield."** Fighting the wrong
  constraint — the inputs *add* stress to a cool crop. The data
  already falsified it (cold bed wins).
- **"Push more fish to bulk the seedlings."** The fish *is* the
  salt; the 26 Apr 10→13 mL/L bump salted the plug right before the
  bad tissue test. Feeding the cause.
- **"Chase the yellow leaves with nitrogen."** N is sufficient;
  yellow is Mg blocked by Na. Treating a symptom with the wrong
  element.
- **"Run lettuce like the tomatoes."** Tomato is warm-season and
  P-lockout-bound; lettuce is cool-season and salt-bound. Same
  greenhouse, opposite crop, different crux.
- **"Set a 200 kg target and call it a plan."** A goal is not a
  strategy.
