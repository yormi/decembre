# Sulfuric acid for water / nutrient-solution pH adjustment — PROHIBITED

## Sulfuric acid for water / nutrient-solution pH adjustment — PROHIBITED (2026-05-29)

**Do not revisit sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄) as a fertigation / irrigation water pH-down agent.** Researched against CAN/CGSB-32.311 (the standard Ecocert Canada certifies against). Cert 4 — direct read of the permitted-substances list + annotation rule; the only residual uncertainty is the in-progress standard revision.

**Finding:**

- H₂SO₄ is **not freely allowed.** It appears in the permitted-substances list under exactly **one** narrow use: pH adjustment of **liquid fish products**, and there only as the **5th-preference** acid — after (1) vinegar, (2) non-synthetic citric acid, (3) synthetic citric acid, (4) phosphoric acid — capped at the **minimum amount needed to stabilize the product.**
- It is **not** listed for lowering fertigation / irrigation water pH, nutrient-solution acidification, or any general crop input.
- **Annotation rule:** a substance may be used only within the scope of the table it is listed under. Off-scope use (water/solution pH-down) is therefore **prohibited**, not merely unlisted.
- Separately: **amino acids hydrolyzed using sulfuric acid are prohibited** in organic crop production. Watch this on any hydrolysate / amino-acid biostimulant label.

**Compliant alternative for water pH lowering:** citric acid. Verify the specific branded product carries an Ecocert / OMRI-Canada review before use — generic "citric acid" is not a blanket pass.

**Caveat:** the 2020 CAN/CGSB-32.311 standard is under revision (2025 public-review draft). Re-check the acid list and the fish-product annotation when the revised standard lands; the prohibition above reflects the 2020 in-force text.
