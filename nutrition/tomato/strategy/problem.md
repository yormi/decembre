# Problem — 2026 tomato decline

## Crux

One impaired root system, not four problems. pH-7.4 calcareous
lockout starves roots of P → small/impaired roots → can't pull N
or Ca → weak tissue → low yield. N-deficiency, Ca-deficiency,
botrytis/leaf-spot, and BER are **faces of root failure**, not
independent causes.

**Two clocks:** root-zone chemistry corrects over months–cycles;
sales need yield now. The strategy must work both (`goal.md`).

## The three faces

| Symptom | Cause |
|---|---|
| Thin new growth | N deficient (2.27% vs 3.10) **+ Ca** (cell-wall; immobile → new growth first) |
| Flower abortion | P −90% (0.23 vs 0.50). NOT boron (B suff); NOT pollination (bumblebees running) |
| Disease (leaf-spot, botrytis) | Weak senescing tissue + Ca below resistance band (3.46 vs 4.5–5.5). Dry house (~48–55% RH) → not climate-driven; **downstream of resiliency = nutrition**. Secondary lever: de-leaf infected lowers (inoculum, not climate). |

## Supply vs uptake

- **P** — the only true supply/pH-lockout case. Upstream: starves
  root mass → throttles N + Ca uptake.
- **K + Ca** — uptake-limited (abundant in solution, short in
  leaf; antagonism ruled out). Feed-side won't help; ride on root
  recovery via P/pH.
- **N** — **uptake-limited**. Feed-side ramp won't help; fix is
  root-bypass (Ocean) + root recovery. cert 4. Reclassification
  trail: `../tomato/learnings/0003-n-reclassified-supply-tight-to-uptake-only.md`.
