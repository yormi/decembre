# N reclassified: "supply-tight + uptake" → uptake-only

2026-06-04. Earlier strategy carried N as a hedge — "both
supply-tight *and* uptake-impaired." The 2026-06-04 SME collapses
the hedge to **uptake-limited only**. Strategy frame:
`nutrition/strategy/problem.md`, `goal.md`, `sg2-nitrogen.md`.

## The reclassification
- **Evidence:** 2026-06-04 SME solution nitrate **174.7 ppm**,
  near the top of band 35–180 → supply is abundant. Tissue still
  **2.27%** → the gap is at the root, not the feed.
- **Replaces:** the prior "both" read, which rested on a
  6-week-stale April SME (cert 3). Fresh SME upgrades the read to
  cert 4.
- **Consequence:** a feed-side N ramp can't help — it adds salt
  to a nitrate pool the impaired root can't pull (CE already past
  the ~3.0 rail at 3.96). The only N lever that delivers is
  **root-bypass** (Ocean amino-N on the AAT/LHT channel), which
  skips the nitrate pump. Same uptake story as K/Ca.
