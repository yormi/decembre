# 2026 tomato decline is one pH-lockout crisis, not three problems

2026-05-30. The reported triad — thin new growth, flower
abortion, botrytis in a dry house — all trace to the single
pH-7.4 lockout (P −90%, N −26%, Ca below resistance band; cert 4
tissue, see `nutrition/tomato/doc/tomato-t5-tissue-analysis-2026-05-23.md`).
Strategy frame: `nutrition/strategy/problem.md` + `nutrition/strategy/goal.md`.

## Decisions
- **Botrytis is a secondary symptom, not a target.** It's
  downstream of the P+Ca crisis (rot colonises weak/aborted
  tissue + unhealed scars). The 2026-05-28 anti-botrytis pivot
  (cutting fertigation K/Mg) was treating the symptom — flag for
  the next `/retire-recipe`, the cation cuts may be mis-aimed.
- **Flower abortion is NOT boron** — tissue B 57 ppm sufficient.
  Considered B (textbook abortion symptom); rejected on tissue
  evidence. It's the P/energy starvation. Don't buy borax.
- **Thin new growth is N *and* Ca, not N alone** — Ca builds the
  cell-wall pectate mortar and is immobile, so deficiency hits
  new growth first. Stem strength rides on Ca, so SG3 (Ca) is
  not a botrytis-only subgoal.
- **N target is ~4–4.5%, not the 3.1% floor** — the lab floor is
  the exit-crisis line, not the vigour line; full-production
  indeterminate tomato wants mid-upper band.
- **Priority = root-cause first, not speed first.** P starvation
  limits root mass, throttling N + Ca uptake, so P is upstream.
  Subgoals ordered P → N → Ca even though P is slowest
  (multi-cycle pH drawdown). Considered speed-first
  (N → Ca → P); rejected the *ranking* but kept the calendar
  insight: all three start now, P runs as a parallel slow clock.
- **Monitoring = tissue tests only.** Sap meter, SPAD, Brix
  rejected as too noisy for decision-grade reads. Pour-through
  soil pH (Bluelab, on hand) kept as the one cheap leading gauge
  for SG1 — it's a soil reading, not a plant proxy.
- **Non-nutrition levers flagged, not owned** — pollination
  (bumblebees/vibration) for abortion; biofungicide (Bacillus /
  Gliocladium, cert-verify) + airflow + dry-morning de-leafing
  for botrytis. Outside specialist lane.

## Why it's worth keeping
Future readers will see N as the obvious "vigour" lever and reach
for it (or boron on the abortion) — this records why P/pH is the
real ceiling, why thin growth is also a Ca story, and why the
ordering is causal not fast-first.
