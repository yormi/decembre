## Cross-cutting signals (only visible across the whole set)

- Disease cluster is overwhelmingly OLD/lower leaves, bottom-up →
  splash/contact fungus establishing in lower canopy, not systemic.
- **Two opposing chlorosis populations.** Most chlorosis is old-growth
  (mobile, e.g. Mg); a minority is new-growth with purple stems
  (immobile/Fe/P). Treating all chlorosis as one deficiency would be a
  mistake — the set cannot reconcile them.
- Fruit disorders split cleanly: coherent physiological Ca (BER/calyx)
  vs a weaker possible fruit-blight group.
- Disease + nutrient stress co-locate on the same lower-canopy leaves —
  stressed senescing tissue is the substrate fungi colonize; the two
  likely reinforce each other.
- Environmental-logger photos show 48 % RH/15 °C and 55 % RH/23-24 °C —
  humidity not obviously high, tempering the leaf-mold hypothesis;
  argues disease is established, not actively exploding.
- Single-photo species calls are unreliable — same lesion variously
  labeled early blight / Septoria / leaf mold. Cross-set pattern is
  solid; species is not.

## Recommended next checks

1. **Resolve the fungus** — hand-lens/scope the lesion UNDERSIDES on
   5-10 lower leaves: olive concentric rings = Alternaria; black
   pycnidia = Septoria; olive-brown fuzz = leaf mold (Passalora). One
   check settles the biggest open question.
2. **Full tissue test** (recently-mature leaf): Mg, K, Ca, Fe, Mn, N,
   P, B — resolves the Mg-vs-Fe-vs-N conflict + the new/old-growth
   split. Do not act on a nutrient until tissue confirms.
3. **Soil/substrate** — pH + Mehlich-3 for K:Mg ratio and P
   availability (lead with M3 + tissue for soil; SME ranges don't apply
   to soil). High pH explains an Fe story; wide K:Mg explains Mg.
4. **Irrigation-consistency + Ca audit** — confirm the BER/calyx
   cluster is supply/uptake-driven, not transient.
5. **Scout fruit** for more blight-type lesions + stem cankers near
   infected fruit — harvest-risk decision.
6. **Whitefly scouting** — yellow sticky-card counts + underside
   inspection; decide on biocontrol (Encarsia / swirskii).
7. **Review last 7-10 days** of foliar spray log + weather/light swings
   to confirm or rule out the bleached-shoot phytotoxicity read.
8. **Organic status** — confirm CAN/CGSB-32.311 for any control product
   before recommending (Ecocert-allowed copper/sulfur/biofungicide);
   don't assume.

## Caveats

- Photos diagnose pattern, not species or exact element. Confidence ≥4
  only on the fungal-complex *pattern*; everything else needs the
  confirmatory checks above.
- 2 photos unusable; 3 were healthy negatives (problems are not
  whole-plant systemic collapse).
