**Visual differential (no diagnosis assigned — field/lab confirmation needed):**
- *Botrytis cinerea* stem canker — most common greenhouse tomato stem disease; tan/brown elongated lesion, often originates at a pruning wound, gray sporulation in humid conditions (not visible here, possibly too early or too dry).
- *Didymella lycopersici* (Didymella stem canker) — darker brown, often with concentric rings + small black pycnidia; less common in QC greenhouses.
- *alternaria solani* stem canker — concentric rings typical; usually starts on lower stem near soil line.
- Bacterial canker (*Clavibacter michiganensis*) — usually presents with wilting + vascular browning on cut + leaf marginal scorch, not isolated stem lesion. Less likely from this photo alone.
