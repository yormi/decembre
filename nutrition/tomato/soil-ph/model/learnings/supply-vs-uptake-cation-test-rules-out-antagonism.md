**Status:** accepted

(`SME - 2026-04-10.pdf` report 39087, `tissus - 2026-05-22.pdf`

## The cation test — why it is NOT antagonism

- **K is the aggressor** in cation antagonism — it suppresses Ca,
  Mg, NH₄, not the reverse. Nothing here out-competes K.
- Yet K, in abundant supply (292), reaches the leaf only
  marginally. **If the winner also fails, antagonism cannot be the
  explanation** — antagonism is relative (one ion beating another).
- N, K, Ca all fall short *together* despite abundant solution
  levels → one shared cause at the root, not competition between
  them. → **impaired active uptake.** (cert 3)

## Corrections logged (this supersedes prior framing)

- **Sulfate is NOT a Ca limiter.** Solution Ca 238 ppm is *above*
  optimal. Gypsum (CaSO₄) equilibrium only caps solution Ca near
  saturation — but that cap sits at ~240 ppm, more than the plant
  needs. Sulfate's real issues are salinity (CE) + being a marker
  of input overload, not Ca starvation. (Retracts the earlier
  "sulfate is a third squeeze on Ca" read.)
- **The Ca-gap "antagonism half"** named in
  [[what-the-ph-lever-actually-addresses-per-t5-tissue]] is
  demoted: with solution Ca abundant and K (the antagonizer) itself
  failing to reach the leaf, the Ca shortfall is **uptake**, not
  K/Mg antagonism. Same conclusion that file reaches by another
  route (uptake-side), but the antagonism mechanism is the wrong
  label.

## Form constraint (why we keep sulfate K/Mg)

Every organic-allowed **non-sulfate** soluble K or Mg form is
carbonate/oxide → **alkalizing** (K-bicarbonate, wood ash,
dolomite, MgO), wrong for a high-pH lockout. Muriate (KCl) and
KNO₃ are prohibited. So K₂SO₄ + MgSO₄ are the correct *form*; the
lever is total *quantity* (salinity), not form — and that trim is
already spent per the cross-ref file.

## Water uptake intact ≠ nutrient uptake intact (2026-05-31)

The transpiration evidence (leaf-cooler-than-air + tensiometer
swing → live water-conducting roots) proves the **water** job,
not the **nutrient** job. Two different root functions:

- **Move water** — passive bulk-flow, driven by transpiration
  tension; works as long as xylem is live. (proven intact)
- **Take up ions** — active, energy + transporters + absorptive
  surface; degraded by P-starved root growth + high-pH /
  bicarbonate stress on the transporters. (impaired)

So "roots transpire enough to cool leaves" does NOT certify
nutrient-uptake capacity, and the transpiration read was only
ever deployed to rule out rot/aeration — not to certify root
mass. pH is the root cause; its proximate effect on N/K/Ca/Mg
uptake runs through (a) too little functional absorptive root
and (b) direct high-pH/bicarbonate transporter stress — both
downstream of pH, not competing with it. (cert 3)

Consequence: the impairment is partly **direct-pH**, not only
mineral availability. So transiently lowering rhizosphere pH
(feed-water acid) *can* nudge uptake — but on free-carbonate
soil it re-buffers to ~7.4 within minutes per pulse, so the
window is a small duty-cycle, not a hold. Buffer paradox: the
harder the carbonate buffer (more reason to want acid), the
shorter each transient lasts. Durable fix stays bulk-pH
drawdown (S) regrowing root surface. See
[[carbonate-gates-ph-correction-feasibility]] (citric row).

## Non-pH uptake rivals — status (2026-05-31)

- **Aeration** — ruled out (tensiometer swing, above).
- **Root-zone temperature** — cleared. Measured ~17 °C; above the
  ~15–16 °C where cold gates tomato active uptake. Mildly below
  optimum (~20 °C) so a small drag at most, not a cause.
- **Salinity / bicarbonate / P-stunted root mass** — same carbonate
  event in other hats; ease when pH is corrected, not independent.
- **Residual pathogen** — CLOSED. Dug roots are white/healthy
  (2026-05-31), no rot. Lockout is functional/chemical, not
  structural root damage.
- **Surface-preferential rooting** — roots observed white but
  concentrated at the surface under the landscape fabric. Partly
  normal plasticulture behaviour, but shallow-preferential rooting
  is a likely *avoidance* signal: healthy roots clustering where the
  profile is least hostile (top = fertigation water + air) while the
  deeper carbonate/high-pH/salinity zone is shunned. Healthy-but-
  shallow → reduced soil volume explored → reduced total uptake even
  with good tissue. OPEN: is there a deep white root system too, or
  is rooting mostly the surface mat? If mostly shallow, pH
  correction must reach depth, not just the top. (cert 2)
