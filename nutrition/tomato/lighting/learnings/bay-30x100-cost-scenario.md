# Worked scenario — lighting a 30×100 ft tomato bay


One instance of the [[domain]] energy/cost laws, for the VYPR 2x at the listing in [[derivation]].


## Scenario inputs

- Fixture: Fluence **VYPR 2x** — 900 µmol/s, 2.6 µmol/J, 346 W

- Bay: 30 × 100 ft = 3,000 ft² = **279 m²** (2.79M cm²)

- Photoperiod: **16 h/day**

- Energy price: **$0.06/kWh**

- Delivery efficiency: ~85%


## Result

| Target PPFD | Fixtures | Electrical cost | Canopy light |
|---|---|---|---|
| 200 µmol/m²/s | ~73 | $24/day | 250 J/cm²/day |
| 300 µmol/m²/s | ~109 | $36/day | 375 J/cm²/day |
| 400 µmol/m²/s | ~146 | $49/day | 500 J/cm²/day |

- Monthly (daily, 16 h): ~$730–1,500.

- Canopy light ÷ electrical draw ≈ 48%; ~52% is heat to vent.
