# Protocol — salt flush (leaching)


## Trigger

- Read pour-through EC weekly (each feed-day) → flush when > 1.5 mS/cm.

- Backstop: never go > 2 feeds without an EC check.


## Steps

1. Make sure trays never sit in their own leachate (Na re-wicks up).

2. Apply plain tap water, no feed → slowly, per container below.

   | Container | Water per pass | Time @ ~8 L/min wand |
   |---|---|---|
   | 50-cell tray | ~3–4 L | ~25–30 s |
   | 32-cell tray | ~4–5 L | ~30–40 s |
   | 3″ pot | ~0.5–0.6 L | ~5 s |
   | 4″ pot | ~0.8–1.1 L | ~8 s |


3. Drain fully → discard leachate to waste.

4. If EC was > 3 : wait 30min and repeat. Otherwise, wait tomorrow and re-read Pour-through EC


## Notes

- From 5+ mS/cm, one pass won't reach band → expect multiple passes (4–5× cut).

- Space passes, don't flood. Drain fully + partial dry-back between each (≥30–60 min). Occluded salt diffuses out slowly. 2 passes/day over 2–3 days beats 5 back-to-back.

- Same-day post-flush EC reads low (rebound overnight as trapped salt re-equilibrates) → confirm next morning before declaring ≤ 1.6.

- Salt-sensitive roots — never leave plugs saturated all day (anoxia/rot).
