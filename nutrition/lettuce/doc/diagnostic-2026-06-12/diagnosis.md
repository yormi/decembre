## The through-line holds on field plants

Same diagnosis as 06-06, now on the live crop, not just plugs:

- **Tip-burn is everywhere** (23/33) — brown/tan dead margins and
  tips on otherwise-green tissue, worst on exposed and young wavy
  leaves. This is the visible face of **Ca starvation at the leaf
  edge**, driven by Na blocking Ca and heat mis-sending Ca to outer
  leaves. It is the quality tax the 06-06 read predicted.

- **Salt bronzing, not just variety** (15 photos) — dull red-brown
  spreading onto green leaves and leaf margins, beyond the clean
  lustrous red of healthy red salanova. The agents were told to treat
  red-variety pigment as normal; they flagged stress only where it was
  dull, marginal, or paired with necrosis.

- **Over-aged crowded plugs** (13 photos) — dense interlocking
  canopy, étiré leaves reaching for light, plants well past tray-out
  size. Same propagation ceiling as 06-06.


## Two new signals — at the crowded hot core

Neither appeared in the 06-06 nursery set. Both are **low-to-moderate
confidence and need a closer look** (hand-lens / closer shot) before
acting:

- **Wet rot / mold at the core** — `img_4672` (3/5), `img_4674`
  (2/5), `img_4675` (3/5), `img_4694` (3/5). Soft collapsing inner
  leaves at the collar/core of the densest tray plants — consistent
  with a hot, closed, humid canopy, not a primary pathogen. Distinct
  from red pigment: look for translucent brown-tan **soft** tissue.

- **Chewing damage** — `img_4685` (2/5). Holes / leaf perforations
  from a defoliator (slug or caterpillar). Single photo, low
  confidence.

These read as **consequences of the same crowded-and-hot condition**,
not independent problems: pack salt-/heat-stressed plants tight and the
core stays wet → rot starts; soft stressed tissue is easier chewing.


## Confirm before acting

- **Rot vs pigment** — on the 4 flagged photos, check whether the
  brown core tissue is soft/wet (rot) or just dark red (pigment). A
  closer shot or a touch test settles it.

- **Pest** — one low-confidence chewing read; scout the bed for
  slugs/caterpillars rather than treating off one photo.

- **The dominant lever is unchanged** — tip-burn + salt bronzing
  point back to the 06-06 strategy crux (cool the crop, cut the ocean
  feeds, fix the plug), not to a spray. Rot/pest are secondary to the
  crowded-hot microclimate.
