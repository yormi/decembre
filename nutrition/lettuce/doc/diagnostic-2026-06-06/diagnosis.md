## Synthesis — working diagnosis

**One line: a cool-season crop run hot and fed the ocean.** Salanova
is salt-stressed and heat-stressed across its whole life — sodium is
the through-line, heat is the accelerant. The head never beats 60 g
because the head-building machinery can't run under salt + heat.

The plant is hit at every stage:
- **Salted in the nursery** — three marine feeds (Ocean + Acadie
  Poisson fish 13 mL/L + kelp), concentrated in 1.25 L cells with no
  leaching → seedling Na 3166.
- **Planted salted into salty soil** — marine shrimp compost +
  sulphur-program CE creep → transplant Na 4443.
- **Run too warm at both stages** — hot propagation (bolt/mold/
  decline) + hot field (bolt/bronze; heat lifts calcareous pH 6.9→7.3
  → micro lockout, and drives the transpiration that pulls in more Na).

### Three factors, ranked

| Factor | Role | Where | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sodium / salinity** | through-line — blocks Ca/K/Mg, stalls growth | nursery feed **and** field soil (two ocean sources) | 3.5–4/5 |
| **Heat** | accelerant — bolt, bronze, raises pH→lockout, drives Na uptake | propagation **and** field | 4–5/5 |
| **Transplant quality** | sets the ~60 g ceiling — over-aged, pre-bolt, stalled plug | propagation | 3.5/5 |

Not independent: **salt + heat *cause* the bad plug** (it bolts/
declines because it's salted, cooked, crowded in 50-cell, held past
the 4-wk peak). Fix salt + heat in propagation and the plug fixes
itself.

### Sodium — sources + mechanism

- **Two ocean sources, not the bed soil alone:** nursery = fish +
  kelp feed concentrated in small cells (fish bumped 10→13 mL/L on
  26 Apr "to push tray-finishing" — salted the plug right before the
  14 May test); field = marine shrimp compost + CE creep. Water is
  clean (EC 0.10) — salt is not from irrigation.
- **Three damage mechanisms at once:** (1) **osmotic** — salt lowers
  water pull, plant can't drink in damp medium → stall/stunt;
  (2) **ionic toxicity** — Na⁺ piles up at leaf margins, jams K⁺
  enzyme sites → marginal scorch/tipburn, costs ATP to pump out;
  (3) **antagonism** — Na⁺ blocks K⁺/Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ uptake + strips Ca off
  the root membrane → induced Ca/K/Mg deficiency (the tissue signature)
  → soft walls, tipburn, interveinal yellowing. **Ca is the keystone:
  Na blocks it and causes the damage Ca prevents.**

Symptom → cause map:

| Symptom (operator list) | Face of |
|---|---|
| Feuilles jaunes | Mg deficiency + lockout (NOT N) |
| Feuilles molles | low Ca/K (soft tissue) + heat push |
| Pucerons | aphids on soft heat-stressed tissue |
| Feuilles allongées | heat-driven stretch / pre-bolt |
| Externes meurent au transplant | over-aged bolting plug + Ca deficiency |
| Uniformité planches | uneven declining seedlings |
| Blanc | hot crowded humid trays (mildew/mold) |
| Stall 2 semaines | transplanting a spent pre-bolt plug |

## Open questions / confirmatory checks

- **Gross vs trimmed 60 kg — RESOLVED: gross/harvest, untrimmed.**
  Heads are physically small → the primary loss is failure to bulk
  (osmotic salt-stall + heat + short window), not tipburn cull.
  Tipburn/Ca is a quality tax on top, not the main ceiling.
- **Feed Na test** — confirm the nursery salt source: send the
  as-mixed feed (Acadie Poisson 13 mL/L + kelp 2 mL/L) + a plug
