# "Crowding" was over-aging — but the plug vehicle is still a lever

Decided 2026-06-07, revised 2026-06-13, building the salanova growing
protocol (`../goal.md`). Two separate points that got conflated the first
time round — now untangled.

## Point 1 — "crowding" reclassified (stands)

The diagnosis (`../problem.md`) listed four nursery faults: salted, cooked,
crowded, over-aged. **"Crowded" was a symptom of over-aged, not an
independent cause.** A small cell only reads as crowded once roots outgrow
it — and they outgrew it *because the plug was held to 5–8 wk*, past the
4-wk peak. Fix pull-age and the small-cell crowding never appears. Four
faults → three (salted · cooked · over-aged).

## Point 2 — vehicle IS a lever for plug WEIGHT (revised)

The first pass concluded "keep 50-cell, cell size isn't a lever." **That was
wrong, and farm data overturned it:** a **3″ pot** produced a **40 g plug in
5 wk** (4 wk targeted), vs ~16 g in the 50-cell tray. Cell size is not the
*crowding* lever — it's the **head-start** lever. The new protocol uses the
**3″ pot**:

- **40 g advanced plug** → short bed flip → less heat-exposed time → less
  bolt risk (the summer play — see `../goal.md` § bolt clock).
- **More media volume → salt buffering returns.** The salt fix is therefore
  **no longer 100% Stage 4** — the 3″ pot's media dilutes feed-salt
  concentration; Stage-4 leaching + the bigger cell now share the load.
- Cost: far fewer plugs per bench than 50-cell. Accepted for plug quality.

## Why both points coexist

Crowding-as-over-aging (Point 1) is a statement about the *past failure*
(why the 50-cell plug went bad: it was held too long). The 3″-pot choice
(Point 2) is about the *future protocol* (build a heavy head-start plug).
They don't conflict: we're not bumping pot size to cure crowding, we're
bumping it to ship a 40 g plug and buffer salt.

## Rejected

- **Keep 50-cell** — bench-efficient but ships a light ~16 g plug → longer
  bed window → more summer bolt exposure. Lost to the 3″ pot's head-start.
- **Intermediate (32-cell)** — a ~25–30 g compromise; unnecessary once the
  3″-pot 40 g result was already in hand.
