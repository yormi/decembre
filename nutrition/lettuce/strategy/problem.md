# Salanova — diagnosis

Strategy kernel 1/3 (Rumelt: **diagnosis → guiding policy →
coherent action**). Policy + actions: `goal.md`. Evidence:
`../doc/diagnostic-2026-06-06/diagnosis.md`.

## The challenge

- Best ever **60 kg/bed** (≈60 g/head × 1000) vs target **200 kg**
  (≈200 g/head). Gap = triple the head — real mass, heads are
  physically small, not trimmed. The plant isn't bulking.

- Bed 23.2 m², 1000 heads ≈ 43/m².

- Paradox: the 60 kg came from the **cold, no-input** bed. Heat +
  CO₂ + light did *worse*. Premium inputs are net-negative.

## The crux

**The bottleneck is the plug — crap before it reaches the field.**
A weak, over-aged, bolting plug can't make a 200 g head; the field
only loses from there. The cold no-input bed proves it (best field
+ crap plug = 60 g).

Three compounding propagation faults:

- **Salted** — three marine feeds (Ocean + fish 13 mL/L + kelp)
  concentrate in small cells, no leaching → seedling Na 3166, tray
  EC 1.9–2.6 (want ~1.0–1.5). The 26 Apr fish bump (10→13 mL/L)
  worsened it.

- **Cooked** — propagation too warm for a cool crop → bolting +
  mold in trays.

- **Over-aged** — held 5–8 wk though weight peaks at **4 wk** then
  declines (16→10→12 g); transplanted on the way down, already
  flowering. (Roots outgrow the cell from the hold, not cell size
  — `learnings/crowding-is-overaging-not-cell-size.md`.)

The field then **finishes** it: salty calcareous soil (marine
shrimp compost + CE creep → Na 4443, Ca/K/Mg blocked), run hot (pH
6.9→7.3 lockout, more Na uptake) → 2-wk stall, no bulking.

**Mechanism = sodium + heat.** Sodium is the through-line (nursery
feed + field soil, two ocean sources); heat the accelerant at both
stages. Na hits three ways: **osmotic** (can't drink → stall),
**ionic** (margin scorch), **antagonism** (blocks Ca/K/Mg →
tipburn, soft, yellow). Dominant loss = osmotic stall + short
bulking window of a bolt-committed plug; tipburn is a quality tax,
not the ceiling.

## Critical vs noise

- **Critical:** sodium, heat, plug quality.

- **Not nitrogen** — "feuilles jaunes" = Mg blocked by Na (tissue N
  3.87 sufficient).

- **Not light-short** — light helps deep winter only; warm season
  it just adds heat.

- **Not P** — lettuce P fine/high (unlike tomato).

- **Not pollination/variety** — environment + feed.

## Supply vs uptake

- **Ca/K/Mg — uptake-BLOCKED by Na**, not supply-short. Lever = cut
  Na + protect Ca.

- **N — sufficient.** More fish-N is negative (fish = the salt).

- **P — adequate.**

- **The stall** is osmotic + short window, not a missing nutrient.

## Two clocks

- **Nursery de-salt + plug fix — FAST** (next sowing).

- **Field soil-salt drawdown — SLOW** (leaching, weeks–months;
  shares tomato soil clock). Don't wait on it.

## Bad strategy — traps that feel like action

- **Add heat / CO₂ / light** — adds stress to a cool crop; data
  already falsified it (cold bed wins).

- **Push more fish** — the fish *is* the salt.

- **Chase yellow with N** — N sufficient; yellow is Mg-block.

- **Run it like the tomatoes** — tomato warm-season P-lockout;
  lettuce cool-season salt-bound. Different crux.

- **Set a 200 kg target and call it a plan** — a goal isn't a
  strategy.

## Open gates

- **Feed Na test** — as-mixed feed + plug pour-through; ask Na + Cl
  + Ca/K/Mg.

- **Good-plug trial** — 4-wk cool plug → cold soil vs clean media;
  separates plug ceiling from soil ceiling.

Confidence: heat-as-depressor 4–5/5; sodium through-line 3.5–4/5
(raised by the June field SME — root-zone CE 4.01, up from April 1.08,
salt now solution-confirmed: `../learnings/field-sme-salinity-climbed-2026-06.md`);
plug-as-ceiling 3.5/5.
