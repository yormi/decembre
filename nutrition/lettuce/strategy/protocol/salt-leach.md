# Protocol — salanova bed salt leach (field reclamation)

Drainage half of the sulphur program: sulphur titrates carbonate → CaSO₄; leaching removes that sulfate + nitrate + excess Ca so root-zone EC stays under the salt guardrail. Salt is saline-not-sodic (June SME RAS 1.29) → plain water leaches it, no gypsum amendment needed.

Trigger basis: `../../learnings/field-sme-salinity-climbed-2026-06.md`. EC bands + conversion: `soil-ph-ec-test.md`. Guardrail: `../../soil-ph/model/derivation.md`.


## Trigger

- 1:1 slurry EC > **0.65 dS/m** (≈ ECe 1.3, the salt gate) → leach.

- Recheck before every transplant + every sulphur pass.


## Pre-check (else leaching relocates salt, not removes)

- Confirm free drainage below the root zone (`../../../../protocol/bed-drainage-test.md`) — no perched water table, no hardpan at ~20 cm. If water perches, salt wicks back up; fix drainage first.

- Pair the EC read with lab Na/sulfate (Berger subset) → attribute self-applied sulfate vs sodium. High sulfate + low RAS = leachable; rising Na/RAS = a different problem.


## Steps

1. Measure baseline 1:1 slurry EC (`soil-ph-ec-test.md`), 5-spot composite.

2. Pre-wet bed to field capacity with plain water (no feed).

3. Apply leaching water in excess, plain, ~**30–50 L/m² per pass** → let it drain through the root zone.

4. Drain fully → partial dry-back (root zone not waterlogged).

5. Re-read 1:1 EC next morning (same-day reads rebound).

6. Repeat passes until EC ≤ 0.65 (≈ ECe 1.3). From 4 dS/m expect **cumulative ~100 L/m²** over 2–3 passes (cert 1, FAO reclamation rule — water depth ≈ soil depth removes ~80 % of mobile salt; intermittent ~halves it).


## Sulphur interaction

- Leach **before** the next sulphur pass so the gypsum-EC pulse lands on a bed under the guardrail, not over it.

- Continue the sulphur program — its sulfate is leachable and low-SAR; it is not the salinity problem, only an EC contributor.


## Maintenance

- Hold a leaching fraction of ~**20–30 %** on routine fertigation (`../fertigation-recipe.md` targets the same) → keeps salt moving down, EC steady.

- Salt-sensitive roots — never hold the bed saturated all day (anoxia / crown rot).
