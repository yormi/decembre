# Canopy-closure timing — provenance + confidence

Provenance for the closure-timing figures in `../domain.md` (`### Growth rate`, `### Canopy closure`), which states truths only.

All figures below are **cert 1–2 (low)** — first-principles, not yet observed on Décembre's trays.


## First closure ~3 wk from sowing

Salanova, 32-cell tray, 3″ pots, under lamps + heating.

Reasoned: germination + cotyledon (~d0–4) → slow rosette build (wk 1–2) → adjacent rosettes touch at 32-cell pitch (~wk 3). Not timed on-site.


## Re-closure ~5–10 d after a density split

Reasoned from growth-rate physics: established plant at peak expansion needs ~1 leaf-area doubling to refill ×√2 spacing; healthy leaf area doubles every ~5–7 d at this stage. Not timed on-site.


## Lamp DLI floor ~11.5 mol/m²/d

200 µmol/m²/s × 16 h photoperiod, lamps only. Photoperiod assumed 16 h — confirm against actual fixture schedule. Lands in the seedling DLI target band (10–12), which is why winter lamp-only light hits target.


## Season-flatness of closure timing

Claim: lamps pin DLI, heating pins night temp → closure timing season-independent, residual is summer-fast. Logical consequence of the two pins, not measured. Residual summer acceleration (sun overshoots DLI target + warmer uncapped days) unquantified.
