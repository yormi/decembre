# Inner-heart tipburn DLI constants — provenance + confidence

Provenance + confidence for the three constants in the night-form critical-DLI relation, kept out of `../domain.md` (`### Calcium & inner-heart tipburn`), which states truths only.

Relation: `DLI_critical ≈ DLI₀ + c · HD_house_night + Ca_foliar`.

All three are **cert 1–2 (low)** and **borrowed, not fitted to Décembre's beds**.


## DLI₀ ≈ 11 mol/m²/d

Ca floor from root pressure / guttation at zero transpiration — the DLI a butterhead tolerates with essentially no transpirational Ca delivery (humid still night).

First-principles + literature estimate. Not measured on-site.


## c ≈ 2–3 (mol/m²/d)/(g/m³), house-night-HD basis

Night transpiration→Ca coupling slope. **Doubly derived, not fitted:**

- Re-derived from an earlier daytime/house-scale fit (HD 1.5 / 3.7 / 7.4 g/m³ → DLI 13 / 17 / 22, slope ~1.5 on a wide daytime axis).

- Re-scoping to NIGHT heart HD shrank the operative range to ~0–3.5 g/m³, forcing a steeper slope (~3–4 heart basis).

- Converting to the measurable house-HD axis via `f_coupling` (~0.7 under strong HAF) gives ~2–3.

**Not a fixed constant** — scales with HAF strength because it absorbs `f_coupling`.


## f_coupling ~0.1–0.3 (still air) → ~0.6–0.9 (strong HAF at head level)

Ratio night-heart-HD ÷ night-house-HD. Physical reasoning about boundary-layer flushing of the enclosed heart pocket; lower for innermost wrapped leaves. No measurement.

Now folded into `c`, not a standalone model input.
