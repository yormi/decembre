# Growth-rate doubling times — provenance + confidence

Provenance + confidence for the doubling-time table in `../domain.md`
(`### Growth rate`), which states the relation but not how sure we are.

All values are **cert 1–2 (low)** — first-principles + literature,
**not fitted to Décembre's beds**. Same shelf as the tipburn DLI constants.


## The numbers

| Condition | Doubling time |
|---|---|
| DLI ≥ saturation, 18–20 °C, pre-closure | 6–8 d |
| bed setpoint band (DLI 14–16) | ~8–10 d |
| closure drag or off-band temp | 10–12+ d |

Worked example: 30 g → 100 g = 3.3× = ~1.7 doublings (`log₂(3.3) ≈ 1.74`)
→ ~10–14 d best-case, ~2–3 wk sub-optimal.


## Why cert 1–2

- Doubling time read off generic butterhead/leaf-lettuce RGR under
  light-saturated greenhouse conditions — literature range, not a
  Décembre measurement.

- The DLI-saturation ceiling (~20–25 mol/m²/d) is borrowed from the
  same leaf-expansion saturation already used in the tipburn relation;
  RGR is assumed to share it, not independently fitted.

- "30 g transplant" and "100 g harvest" are nominal whole-plant fresh
  weights, soil-grown; hydroponic would sit at the fast end.


## What would calibrate

Paired on-site weighings: tag a cohort at a known fresh weight, record
(DLI, 24 h temp, spacing/closure state), re-weigh on a fixed cadence →
fit a doubling time to Décembre's beds instead of borrowing.
